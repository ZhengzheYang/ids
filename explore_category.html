      For each category, the numbers of review vary quite a lot. Restaurants have most reviews of up to 2200 and shopping has 1016 reviews. However, some relatively uncommon business, such as clothes rentals and nanny service has only 1 review. From this plot, we can also find a problem that there is overlap in the business category, which means that one business can be categorized into restaurant and food at the same time, so the number of reviews in both food and restaurant rank as the top two.


      Each category's average score is reported and scatter-plotted. As we can see, some categories of businesses have generally lower ratings, such as some repair/moving/inspection services. Most high-end business tend to have higher ratings.


      The distribution of the average ratings in each category seems to be more or less similar. Each category seems to have businesses that are rated higher or lower. The disparity between categories is shown more obviously on the numbers of ratings, since some the category have fewer customers who would leave reviews, such as currency exchange and elementary schools.
